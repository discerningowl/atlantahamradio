How Public‑Service Ham Radio Supports Races, Parades & Events

For event organizers, sponsors, and municipalities

Why Use Ham Radio?

Amateur radio operators provide a proven, reliable communications backbone for public events of any size. Here's what makes ham radio an ideal choice for event communications:

  • Independent, reliable voice & data mesh – Works even when cellular or Wi‑Fi is overloaded or down.
  • Off‑grid power – Battery, solar, or generator‑backed radios keep communications alive for hours.
  • Rapid deployment – A portable repeater or mobile command station can be set up in less than 30 minutes and moved as the route changes.
  • Direct link to emergency services – Trained operators speak the same frequencies as police, fire, EMS, and local officials.
  • Real‑time logging – Every call, incident, and resource movement is recorded for post‑event analysis.

How to Get Started

  1. Contact your local amateur‑radio club
    Most have a public‑service committee ready to support community events.
  2. Define the event scope
    Outline your timeline, route, expected participants, and any special hazards.
  3. Secure a shared frequency
    Coordinate with the club and local authorities (typically VHF 146 MHz or UHF 440 MHz).
  4. Integrate into your Incident Command System
    Assign a liaison officer to the ham team to ensure smooth coordination.
  5. Run a pre‑event test
    Verify coverage, power, and interoperability with emergency services before event day.
